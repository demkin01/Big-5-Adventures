Discover Aberdare National Park

Aberdare National Park, nestled in Kenya’s central highlands, offers a unique blend of misty forests, dramatic waterfalls, and high moorlands. Famous for Treetops Lodge, where Queen Elizabeth II ascended the throne, Aberdare is a haven for rare wildlife and scenic treks.

With its cool climate, diverse ecosystems, and historical significance, Aberdare is perfect for adventurers seeking a serene and immersive safari experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Aberdare National Park safe for visitors?

Yes, Aberdare is safe with experienced guides and rangers. Follow safety guidelines during treks and game drives for a secure experience.

How far is Aberdare from Nairobi?

Aberdare is about 160 km (2-3 hours’ drive) from Nairobi, accessible via a scenic route through central Kenya.

What should I pack for an Aberdare safari?

Pack warm clothing for the cool highland climate, waterproof gear, sturdy hiking boots, binoculars, a camera, and insect repellent.

Is malaria a concern in Aberdare?

Malaria risk is lower in Aberdare due to its high altitude, but precautions like insect repellent and long sleeves at dusk are recommended.

What’s the best time to visit Aberdare?

The dry seasons (June-October, January-February) are ideal for trekking and wildlife viewing. Wet seasons (November-December, March-May) offer lush scenery.

Can I stay at Treetops Lodge?

Yes, Treetops Lodge is open to guests, offering unique wildlife viewing and historical charm, bookable through our packages.
