Discover Amboseli

Amboseli National Park, located in southern Kenya, is renowned for its large elephant herds and breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest peak. Covering 392 square kilometers, the park’s swamps, fed by underground springs from Kilimanjaro, create a unique ecosystem supporting diverse wildlife.

Famous for its accessibility and open savannahs, Amboseli offers exceptional opportunities for wildlife viewing, photography, and cultural experiences with the Maasai community. It’s a must-visit for those seeking a compact yet spectacular safari experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Amboseli safe for tourists?

Yes, Amboseli is generally safe for tourists. Professional guides conduct all game drives, and the park is well-patrolled. Follow your guide’s instructions to ensure safety around wildlife.

Can I see Mount Kilimanjaro year-round?

Mount Kilimanjaro is visible on clear days, particularly during the dry seasons (June-October, January-February). Cloud cover during rainy seasons may obscure views.

What should I pack for an Amboseli safari?

Pack neutral-colored clothing, comfortable shoes, a warm jacket for mornings, sun protection (hat, sunscreen), binoculars, a camera with a zoom lens, and travel documents. Lodges provide laundry services.

Is malaria a concern in Amboseli?

Yes, malaria is present. Take prophylaxis (consult your doctor), use insect repellent, and sleep under mosquito nets provided by lodges. Risk is higher during rainy seasons.

What’s the best time to visit Amboseli?

The dry seasons (June-October, January-February) are ideal for wildlife viewing and clear Kilimanjaro views. The wet seasons (November-December, March-May) offer lush scenery and fewer crowds.

How long should I stay in Amboseli?

A minimum of 2-3 nights is recommended for a fulfilling experience, allowing multiple game drives. Photographers or those seeking a deeper experience may prefer 4-5 nights.
