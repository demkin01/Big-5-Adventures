Discover Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park, just 7 km from Nairobi’s city center, offers a unique safari experience where urban and wild worlds collide. Spanning 117 square kilometers, the park is home to four of the Big Five, vibrant birdlife, and stunning savannah landscapes framed by city skyscrapers.

Perfect for day trips or short escapes, Nairobi National Park is ideal for travelers seeking wildlife adventures without leaving the city’s reach. Explore its trails, learn about conservation efforts, and enjoy guided tours tailored to all interests.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Nairobi National Park safe for visitors?

Yes, the park is safe with professional guides and rangers. Follow guide instructions during game drives to ensure safety around wildlife.

How close is the park to Nairobi city?

The park is just 7 km from Nairobi’s city center, a 20–30 minute drive, making it ideal for day trips or short safaris.

What should I pack for a Nairobi safari?

Pack neutral-colored clothing, comfortable shoes, sun protection (hat, sunscreen), binoculars, a camera, and a light jacket for early mornings.

Is malaria a concern in Nairobi National Park?

Malaria risk is low but present. Use insect repellent, wear long sleeves in the evening, and consult your doctor about prophylaxis.

What’s the best time to visit Nairobi National Park?

The dry seasons (June-October, January-February) offer the best wildlife viewing. Wet seasons (November-December, March-May) bring lush scenery and fewer crowds.

Can I visit the park for a day trip?

Yes, Nairobi National Park is perfect for day trips. A 4–6 hour visit allows ample time for game drives and photography.
